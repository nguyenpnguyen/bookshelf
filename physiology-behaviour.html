Physiology of Behaviour

Neil R. Carlson

Physiology of Behavior is a book by Neil R. Carlson that offers a comprehensive and up-to-date overview of behavioral neuroscience, the scientific study of the biological bases of behavior. The book covers the main topics in the field, such as neural and hormonal mechanisms, sensory and motor systems, learning and memory, emotions and motivation, sleep and biological rhythms, psychopathology and treatment, and cognition and language. The book also integrates research findings from human and animal studies, as well as clinical and ethical issues. The book is designed to be accessible and engaging for students, with clear explanations, illustrations, examples, summaries, questions, and activities. The book is suitable for undergraduate and graduate courses in psychology, neuroscience, biology, and related disciplines, as well as anyone interested in learning more about how the brain influences behavior.

Language: English
