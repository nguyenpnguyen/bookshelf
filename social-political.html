Contemporary Social and Political Theory: An Introduction

Fidelma Ashe et al.

Contemporary Social and Political Theory: An Introduction is a book by Fidelma Ashe et al. that provides a clear and comprehensive overview of the main theoretical perspectives and debates in social and political thought since the 1960s. The book examines the impact and implications of new ideas such as feminist theory, poststructuralism, hermeneutics, and critical theory, as well as the challenges they pose to the traditional paradigms of liberalism, Marxism, and rationalism. The book also explores the connections and conflicts between different theories, as well as their relevance for understanding contemporary issues such as democracy, identity, power, and justice. The book is designed to be accessible and engaging for students, with key concepts, examples, summaries, questions, and suggestions for further reading. The book is suitable for undergraduate and postgraduate courses in sociology, politics, philosophy, and related disciplines, as well as anyone interested in learning more about the current state of social and political theory.
