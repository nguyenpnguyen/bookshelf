Cognitive Psychology

Nick Braisby & Angus Gellatly

Cognitive Psychology is a book by Nick Braisby and Angus Gellatly that provides a comprehensive and accessible introduction to the study of cognitive processes, such as perception, attention, memory, language, reasoning, and problem-solving. The book covers the main theories, models, and findings in cognitive psychology, as well as the methods and applications of cognitive research. The book also encourages students to engage with the material through critical thinking questions, activities, and experiments. The book features chapters written by leading experts in the field, edited by experienced instructors who guide the students through the subject in a coherent and logical way. The book is suitable for undergraduate and postgraduate students of psychology and related disciplines, as well as anyone interested in learning more about how the mind works.

Language: English
